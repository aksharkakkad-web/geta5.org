## Per-Unit Research

### Unit 1: Biological Bases of Behavior

**Learning Objectives (PSY-1):**
- PSY-1.A — Explain the relationship between biological factors and behavior/mental processes
- PSY-1.B — Explain the nervous system's role in behavior
- PSY-1.C — Explain how genetics and evolution influence behavior

**Key Topics:**
- Neuron anatomy: dendrites, cell body, axon, myelin sheath, axon terminals, nodes of Ranvier
- Resting potential, action potential, all-or-none principle
- Neurotransmitters: dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine, norepinephrine, GABA, glutamate, endorphins
- CNS vs. PNS; somatic vs. autonomic nervous system; sympathetic vs. parasympathetic
- Brain structures: medulla, pons, cerebellum, reticular activating system, thalamus, hypothalamus, amygdala, hippocampus
- Cerebral cortex: frontal, parietal, temporal, occipital lobes
- Specialized areas: Broca's area (speech production), Wernicke's area (speech comprehension), motor cortex, somatosensory cortex
- Corpus callosum, lateralization, split-brain research
- Brain plasticity (neuroplasticity)
- Research methods: EEG, fMRI, PET, lesion studies, twin studies
- Genetics: chromosomes, genes, dominant/recessive, heritability
- Natural selection, evolutionary perspective
- Sensation: thresholds (absolute/difference), Weber's Law, signal detection theory
- Vision: rods, cones, retina, optic nerve, blind spot, feature detectors
- Hearing: cochlea, basilar membrane, frequency theory, place theory
- Gate control theory, kinesthesis, vestibular sense
- Phantom limb sensation
- Chemical senses: gustation, olfaction, papillae, olfactory bulb
- Sleep: stages (NREM 1-3, REM), circadian rhythms, sleep disorders
- Drugs: depressants (alcohol), stimulants (cocaine, caffeine), hallucinogens (marijuana), opiates
- Tolerance, withdrawal, physical vs. psychological dependence

**Key Psychologists:**
- Roger Sperry — split-brain research
- Paul Broca — Broca's area / speech production
- Carl Wernicke — Wernicke's area / speech comprehension
- Ronald Melzack & Patrick Wall — Gate Control Theory

**Stimulus types:** Text passages (brain damage case studies), tables (neurotransmitter functions)

### Unit 2: Cognition

**Learning Objectives (PSY-2):**
- PSY-2.A — Explain cognitive processes involved in sensation and perception
- PSY-2.B — Explain cognitive processes involved in learning
- PSY-2.C — Explain cognitive processes involved in memory
- PSY-2.D — Explain cognitive processes involved in thinking and problem-solving
- PSY-2.E — Explain cognitive processes involved in language

**Key Topics (Sensation/Perception):**
- Top-down vs. bottom-up processing
- Perceptual set, perceptual constancy
- Gestalt principles: figure/ground, closure, proximity, similarity, continuity
- Depth cues: monocular (linear perspective, texture gradient, interposition, relative size, relative clarity) and binocular (retinal disparity, convergence)
- Selective attention, cocktail party effect
- Change blindness, inattentional blindness
- Apparent movement

**Key Topics (Learning):**
- Classical conditioning: Pavlov, UCS, UCR, CS, CR, acquisition, extinction, spontaneous recovery, generalization, discrimination
- Operant conditioning: Skinner, reinforcement, punishment, schedules (fixed ratio, variable ratio, fixed interval, variable interval)
- Observational learning: Bandura, social learning theory, Bobo doll study
- Insight learning (Kohler), latent learning (Tolman), cognitive maps

**Key Topics (Memory):**
- Multi-store model (Atkinson-Shiffrin): sensory memory, short-term, long-term
- Working memory model: phonological loop, visuospatial sketchpad, central executive
- Levels of processing: structural, phonemic, semantic encoding
- Automatic vs. effortful processing
- Iconic memory, echoic memory
- Long-term memory types: explicit (episodic, semantic), implicit (procedural), prospective
- Encoding, storage, retrieval
- Long-term potentiation
- Forgetting: decay, interference (proactive, retroactive), encoding failure, motivated forgetting
- Retrieval cues, context-dependent memory, state-dependent memory
- Serial position effect, primacy, recency
- Misinformation effect (Loftus)

**Key Topics (Thinking/Problem-Solving):**
- Algorithms vs. heuristics (availability, representativeness)
- Mental set, functional fixedness
- Confirmation bias, belief perseverance
- Framing, anchoring
- Convergent vs. divergent thinking
- Creativity, executive function
- Sunk-cost fallacy, gambler's fallacy
- Schema, prototype, concept, accommodation, assimilation

**Key Topics (Language):**
- Language development: cooing, babbling, one-word stage, two-word/telegraphic stage
- Morphemes, phonemes, semantics, syntax, grammar
- Overgeneralization
- Linguistic relativity (Sapir-Whorf hypothesis)

**Key Psychologists:**
- Ivan Pavlov — classical conditioning
- B.F. Skinner — operant conditioning
- Albert Bandura — observational learning / Bobo doll
- George Miller — 7 ± 2 chunks in STM
- Elizabeth Loftus — misinformation effect / eyewitness memory
- Wolfgang Kohler — insight learning
- Edward Tolman — latent learning / cognitive maps
- Richard Atkinson & Richard Shiffrin — multi-store model

**Stimulus types:** Research study descriptions, tables (memory test results, conditioning schedules)

### Unit 3: Development and Learning

**Learning Objectives (PSY-3):**
- PSY-3.A — Explain how developmental changes affect behavior across the lifespan
- PSY-3.B — Explain how social and emotional development affect behavior

**Key Topics (Physical/Cognitive Development):**
- Lifespan development: continuous vs. discontinuous, nature vs. nurture, stability vs. change
- Prenatal: teratogens, critical periods
- Newborn reflexes: rooting, sucking, grasping, Moro
- Maturation, gross/fine motor coordination
- Visual cliff (Gibson & Walk)
- Piaget's stages: sensorimotor (object permanence), preoperational (animism, conservation, egocentrism, mental symbols, reversibility), concrete operational, formal operational (abstract/hypothetical thinking)
- Theory of mind
- Imprinting, sensitive period, critical period
- Vygotsky: zone of proximal development, scaffolding
- Language acquisition
- Intelligence: fluid vs. crystallized, dementia

**Key Topics (Social/Emotional Development):**
- Attachment: Harlow's monkey research, Ainsworth's strange situation
- Attachment styles: secure, avoidant, anxious-ambivalent, disorganized
- Parenting styles: authoritative, authoritarian, permissive
- Erikson's psychosocial stages (Trust vs. Mistrust through Integrity vs. Despair)
- Marcia's identity statuses: achievement, moratorium, foreclosure, diffusion
- Ecological systems theory (Bronfenbrenner): microsystem, mesosystem, exosystem, macrosystem, chronosystem
- Adolescent development: puberty, primary/secondary sex characteristics, menarche, spermarche
- Social clock, emerging adulthood
- Imaginary audience, personal fable, egocentrism (adolescent)
- Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs)
- Possible selves, identity components (familial, occupational, religious, sexual orientation, racial/ethnic)

**Key Psychologists:**
- Jean Piaget — cognitive development stages
- Lev Vygotsky — zone of proximal development
- Erik Erikson — psychosocial stages
- James Marcia — identity statuses
- Harry Harlow — contact comfort / attachment
- Mary Ainsworth — attachment styles / strange situation
- Diana Baumrind — parenting styles
- Urie Bronfenbrenner — ecological systems theory
- Lawrence Kohlberg — moral development

**Stimulus types:** Research study descriptions (attachment studies, developmental experiments), tables (Erikson stages, Piaget stages)

### Unit 4: Social Psychology and Personality

**Learning Objectives (PSY-4):**
- PSY-4.A — Explain how social context influences behavior and mental processes
- PSY-4.B — Explain how personality is assessed and how it influences behavior

**Key Topics (Social Psychology):**
- Attribution theory: dispositional vs. situational; fundamental attribution error; actor-observer bias; self-serving bias
- Attitudes and persuasion: central vs. peripheral route (elaboration likelihood model), foot-in-the-door, door-in-the-face
- Cognitive dissonance, belief perseverance, confirmation bias
- Social influence: conformity (Asch), obedience (Milgram), compliance
- Normative vs. informational social influence
- Social norms, social roles
- Group dynamics: groupthink, social loafing, social facilitation, deindividuation
- Diffusion of responsibility, bystander effect (Kitty Genovese)
- Prosocial behavior, altruism
- Prejudice, discrimination, stereotypes
- In-group bias, out-group homogeneity effect, ethnocentrism
- Just-world phenomenon, relative deprivation
- Self-fulfilling prophecy, implicit attitudes
- Superordinate goals, multiculturalism, contact hypothesis
- Mere exposure effect
- Attribution styles: optimistic/pessimistic, explanatory style
- Locus of control: internal vs. external

**Key Topics (Motivation and Emotion):**
- Motivational theories: instinct, drive reduction, arousal, incentive, self-determination
- Yerkes-Dodson Law, homeostasis
- Lewin's motivational conflicts: approach-approach, approach-avoidance, avoidance-avoidance
- Sensation-seeking theory
- Emotion theories: James-Lange, Cannon-Bard, Schachter-Singer (two-factor)
- Facial feedback hypothesis
- Display rules, cognitive appraisal
- Hunger: hypothalamus, leptin, ghrelin

**Key Topics (Personality):**
- Psychoanalytic/psychodynamic: Freud — id, ego, superego; unconscious, preconscious; defense mechanisms (repression, denial, regression, projection, displacement, rationalization, reaction formation, sublimation, etc.)
- Projective tests (Rorschach, TAT)
- Humanistic: Maslow — hierarchy of needs; Rogers — unconditional positive regard, self-actualizing tendency
- Social cognitive: Bandura — reciprocal determinism; self-efficacy, self-concept, self-esteem
- Trait theories: Big Five (OCEAN): Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Emotional Stability
- Factor analysis, personality inventories (MMPI, NEO-PI)
- Self-concept, self-esteem, self-efficacy

**Key Psychologists:**
- Solomon Asch — conformity experiments
- Stanley Milgram — obedience experiments
- Sigmund Freud — psychoanalytic theory
- Abraham Maslow — hierarchy of needs
- Carl Rogers — humanistic, person-centered
- Albert Bandura — social cognitive theory
- Stanley Schachter — two-factor theory of emotion
- William James — James-Lange theory of emotion
- Leon Festinger — cognitive dissonance

**Stimulus types:** Research study descriptions (Milgram, Asch experiments), text passages (case scenarios), tables (personality theories)

### Unit 5: Mental and Physical Health

**Learning Objectives (PSY-5):**
- PSY-5.A — Explain how psychological disorders are diagnosed and understood
- PSY-5.B — Explain how psychological disorders are treated
- PSY-5.C — Explain how stress affects physical and mental health

**Key Topics (Psychological Disorders):**
- DSM-5, medical model, biopsychosocial model, diathesis-stress model
- Psychotic disorders: schizophrenia (positive/negative symptoms), schizoaffective disorder, brief psychotic disorder
- Depressive disorders: MDD, persistent depressive disorder
- Bipolar disorders: Bipolar I, Bipolar II, cycling, mania
- Anxiety disorders: specific phobia, agoraphobia, panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, GAD
- OCD and related: OCD, hoarding disorder
- Trauma-related: PTSD (flashbacks, hypervigilance, emotional detachment, insomnia)
- Dissociative disorders: dissociative amnesia, DID
- Feeding/eating disorders: anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa
- Personality disorders: paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal, antisocial, borderline, histrionic, narcissistic, avoidant, dependent, OCPD
- Cultural syndromes: ataque de nervios, taijin kyofusho
- Schizophrenia specifics: hallucinations, delusions (persecution, grandeur), disorganized thinking/speech/behavior, flat affect, catatonia, word salad, dopamine hypothesis
- Phases: premorbid, prodromal, active, residual
- Expressed emotion, diathesis-stress model

**Key Topics (Treatment):**
- Psychodynamic therapy: free association, dream interpretation
- Humanistic therapy: person-centered (active listening, unconditional positive regard)
- Cognitive-behavioral therapies (CBT): cognitive restructuring, cognitive triad, fear hierarchies
- REBT (Ellis), DBT (Linehan)
- Behavioral therapies: systematic desensitization, exposure therapies, aversion therapy, token economies, ABA
- Biomedical: antidepressants, antianxiety, antipsychotics (tardive dyskinesia), lithium
- ECT, TMS, lesioning, psychosurgery (lobotomy)
- Biofeedback
- Group therapy
- Therapeutic alliance, cultural humility
- Ethics: fidelity, nonmaleficence, integrity

**Key Topics (Health Psychology):**
- Stress: stressors, general adaptation syndrome (alarm, resistance, exhaustion)
- Coping strategies
- Burnout
- Industrial-organizational psychology

**Key Psychologists:**
- Aaron Beck — cognitive triad, CBT
- Albert Ellis — REBT
- Joseph Wolpe — systematic desensitization
- Rosenhan — pseudo-patient study (DSM reliability)
- Thomas Szasz — mental illness as myth

**Stimulus types:** Research study descriptions, case vignettes (disorder diagnosis), text passages
