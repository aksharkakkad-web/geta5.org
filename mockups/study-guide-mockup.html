Unit 1 — Biological Bases of Behavior

6 sections · ~20 min read

🧠
Central Theme
Big picture overview
▲
The nervous system coordinates behavior through electrochemical signals. Biological structures shape thought, emotion, and action — understanding neurons, brain regions, and neurotransmitters is the foundation for all of psychology.
📌
Core Concepts
5 concepts to master
▲
Neuron Structure & Function
Dendrites receive signals; axons transmit them. The all-or-nothing principle governs firing threshold.
Synaptic Transmission
Neurotransmitters released into the synapse bind to receptors — excitatory signals increase firing, inhibitory signals decrease it.
Brain Regions & Specialization
Lobes of the cerebral cortex serve distinct functions: frontal (executive), parietal (sensory), temporal (language/memory), occipital (vision).

🧠 Central Theme

The nervous system is the body's command center, using electrochemical signals to coordinate all behavior and mental processes.

Key idea: Biological structures at every level — from individual neurons to large brain regions — shape how we think, feel, and act.
Understanding the neuron's structure and how signals travel across synapses is the foundation for every other unit in AP Psychology.
Exam connection: ~8–10% of the AP exam directly tests nervous system content. Expect scenario-based FRQs linking behavior to specific brain regions.

📖 Key Terms — Expanded View

Neuron — the basic building block of the nervous system; transmits electrochemical signals
Synapse — the gap between neurons where neurotransmitters are released
Action Potential — the electrical impulse that travels down an axon; all-or-nothing principle
Myelin Sheath — fatty coating that speeds neural impulses; damaged in MS
Reuptake — neurotransmitter is reabsorbed by sending neuron; target of many medications
